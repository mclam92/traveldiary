Northern Europe

Northern Europe is filled to the brim with vibrant bustling cities with cultures so rich it will have you feeling a flurry of emotions from the momment you step foot out of the train station. Each city is packed full of many delights, art, music, and a rich history that every traveler who wanders through Europe who wants a change of pace from the south will want to visit!

“Amsterdam is like the rings of a tree: It gets older as you get closer to the center.”

John Green
By the time I had left Paris, I realized that I hadn’t seen much of Northern Europe from trips prior. Though I had loved my time in Germany, there were many parts of the north and that of the Scandinavian countries, I have never considered before. So next, I found myself traveling to the areas surround Hannover and Hamburg in Germany, then followed by a quick visit to Amsterdam and Stockholm. As someone relatively familiar with the industry and the music history foundations northern Germany is home to, Hannover and Hamburg were already well-regarded places I wanted to visit. I wanted to see first-hand the streets and buildings in which many of my historical idols may have walked and lived in, the landscapes in which inspired so many stories and tales, etc. However, although I did get to accomplish my original mission in a sense, I found myself constantly being pulled into new and never-explored directions in my visit to North Europe. On accident, after visiting the Anne Frank House in Amsterdam, I found myself invited to join a canal/boat party during EuroPride Day that year. In Hamburg, I met and started a lifelong friend circle from 6 different nations and we went to the most off-the grid local artist park to learn how to skateboard with local teens. In Hannover, I was invited by local college graduates to explore the summer festival with them. The whole experience in Northern Europe was just thrilling and I think it really just reminded me that the majority of the world’s people are inherently good, and we often forget or overlook this because we find ourselves focusing on the wrong things in our daily lives. Northern Europe was just reinvigorating!
