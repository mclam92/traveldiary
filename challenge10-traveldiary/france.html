France & Genève

France is a country full of conspicuous places. These places not only content the passion of a traveler, but also create an aspiration to live in France. From it's iconic, world class museums, monuments and other attractions in its capital, to its legendary and romantic countryside, this country's rich and diverse country is sure to satisfy all types of travelers!

“France has the only two things towards which we drift as we grow older - intelligence and manners.”

F. Scott Fitzgerald
After self-indulging myself in Italy, I wanted to travel to a place that was more calming and quieter, but still magnificent. I wanted the quaintness and tranquil of the countryside but the options of the diversity and wonder of the cities. It was an obvious choice in heading to France next. Taking a quick overnight bus for five Euros, I found myself getting lost in the little towns, villages, and smaller cities in France. Thought I had been to Paris before, I wanted to see and live the true French lifestyle and enjoy a slower pace in life. Six years in NYC really did a number to me and I think France was the perfect calling at that moment because I wanted to re-inspire myself to love living my life once again. Not that I was in any way having dark or scary thoughts, but I wanted to desire and dream again of my future in a way that seemed to have been lost to me during my time chasing after my career in New York. During my time in France and a few days in Genève, I found myself just marveling at the quiet splendor and beauty that every day and corner had to offer. The sunsets were just sweeter when I could stroll down the streets and enjoy the fresh fragrant air from the public gardens. The ancient castles and white coast cliffs were a calling to my old soul—reminding me of the generations that must have stood in the same spot as me, seeing this exact view. France, outside of Paris, was just a good tonic for reawakening my soul.
